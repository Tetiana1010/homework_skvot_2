Pothos

Inventory | 1914, no.190.
Museum |The Uffizi

Roman statue depicts the mythological figure Pothos, one of the minor gods associated with Aphrodite (Venus), the goddess of love. This statue is a Roman copy of a Greek original by Skopas that was created in 335 BC. Roman marble statue was admired and described by Winckelmann in 1755 during his visit to Rome, in the Gallery of Villa Medici on the Pincian Hill, where it was located at the time.
